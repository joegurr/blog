Category Theory Course

March 2023

"Mathematics is the most beautiful and most powerful creation of the human spirit." - Stefan Banach

I am currently taking a reading course on category theory, and it is hard. The book we are working through is Emily Riehl’s Category Theory in Context.

I think Emily Riehl is one of the coolest people around, but I am struggling through her book. I think it is because I am not a part of the target audience.

While I am taking this reading course I am also taking my first course in abstract algebra. I have done a lot of my own reading and know the basics of Groups, Rings, and Fields. However not yet completing a course in algebra kind of means that I don't really have the prerequisites for Emily's book.

I have found Steve Awodey’s Category Theory a much easier book to read. I am also working through Paolo Aluffi's Algebra: Chapter 0.

My current understanding is that the core concept of category theory is not really categories, or functors, but rather natural transformations, and that the other concepts are defined as a consequence of wanting to understand natural transformations better.

Natural transformations are meant to capture the idea that a transformation does not depend on any arbitrary choices, that it is there ripe for the taking.

I'm enjoying learning category theory, although it is stretching me. Right now we're just about to prove the Yoneda Lemma, which I intend to write about soon.
