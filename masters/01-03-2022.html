Today I read an excellent essay by Nikolai Vavilov called "Reshaping the metaphor of proof". The argument being made is that thinking about a proof as simply text is very wrong. A proof is an interactive, deeply human experience. This article led me to a talk on Univalent Foundations by Vladimir Voevodsky, a Fields medalist. It was a very interesting talk. I completely understand the motivation and the quest for a way to "eliminate" (in some sense) mistakes. I would be incredibly surprised if Univalent Foundations is able to do that. Today was also my first class in Functional Analysis. I am a bit nervous about it.
